    Co-Founder of Caspedia — Ameer M. Alnaimi

    A
    Ameer M. Alnaimi
    Co-Founder · Caspedia

    Ameer M. Alnaimi is the driving force behind Caspedia.uk, having built the entire website from scratch and established it as a free, community-driven information hub. His vision was simple but meaningful: to create a space where students — both within the community and beyond — could document events, celebrate achievements, and access knowledge in a clear and honest format. Ameer serves as the primary editor, appointed referee for break football matches, and the overall steward of Caspedia's direction and standards. We are officially working together for the good of the community.

    Co-Founder of CasJuniors 2027 — Inqiad Haque Arshil

    I
    Inqiad Haque Arshil
    Co-Founder · CasJuniors 2027

    Inqiad Haque Arshil established and manages the official CasJuniors 2027 Instagram page, extending our community's presence beyond this website and into social media. His goal is to grow the community's digital footprint and eventually surpass the reach of CasVoice, the dominant rival Instagram page in the school space. Arshil's work on social media complements Caspedia by broadening awareness and bringing more of the community into the conversation.

    Community Gaming Director — Abdulrahman Al Ahmar

    AA
    Abdulrahman Al Ahmar
    Community Gaming Director

    Abdulrahman Al Ahmar successfully integrated his Minecraft server into the broader community ecosystem, creating a shared virtual space that complements what Caspedia documents in the real world. His initiative has helped increase engagement among members, fostered a spirit of collaboration, and further strengthened the presence and identity of the community as a whole. The Minecraft server stands as one of the more creative expansions of what this community has built together.

    Football Community Initiators — Malek Amro & Yamen Mohammad A.

    MA
    Malek Amro & Yamen Mohammad A.
    Football Community Initiators

    Malek Amro and Yamen Mohammad A. were the ones who first proposed creating a dedicated football channel within Caspedia — a place to formally document the break-time matches, results, and player achievements that might otherwise go unrecorded. Their suggestion led directly to the creation of the Football section of this site, which now serves as the official archive for HaramBall FC and Al Noor FC fixtures. Their initiative is a good example of how community members can shape what Caspedia covers.
